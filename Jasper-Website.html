Overview

As a UI/UX intern for Jasper Financial, I took on a couple of short, additional projects as the summer wrapped up. Because Jasper is a brand new startup, their website needed a revamp, and I volunteered to work on the Jasper blog, product demo request page, and the website landing page. The blog and the demo request were solo projects that I did on my own; the landing page was a joint effort between my partner Hao and me. The website redesign is currently being developed and will be updated on the live site in the Fall of 2020.

Blog Redesign

I was really excited to take on the Jasper blog redesign. This was my first independent project at Jasper, and I ran into some interesting design challenges.

I audited the original blog design that you can see below. It doesn’t look too bad, but there were a couple of areas for improvement that I found.

First, there were no images. One way to drastically improve the user experience is to add visual interest. This is especially important for a blog about dental healthcare financing — it’s hard to keep the reader’s interest when explaining complicated (and, let’s face it, dry) topics.

Solution: I added images to the design. Going forward, writers will have the option to add images to their articles. I made the blog landing page more dynamic by adding a featured story at the top of the screen.

Second, the three-column story layout confuses the eye. On the blog homepage, articles were originally laid out in three columns. I wanted to make the layout simpler and more streamlined.

Solution: I created a single-column layout that is more scannable and adds more white space to the design

Finally, there are three distinct target readers: patients, doctors, and office managers. Jasper’s blog publishes articles for patients looking for healthcare or financing information, dentists and other doctors looking for resources for their practices, and office managers trying to improve their practice. In the old design, each story was tagged with categories like “Dental,” “Practice Resources,” or “Veterinary,” and users could see all stories tagged “Dental.” To see an individual tag, the user had to first scroll until they found a recent article in the category they wanted to view.

Solution: I added a list of all story categories to the top of the blog landing page. Users are able to filter by the tags they want to use immediately to get the most relevant content. At the end of each article, users are shown thumbnails for six other recent stories in the same category (or categories) of the article they just read.

I researched great blogs to get inspiration for my design. I designed a few different low-fidelity concepts before I landed on this one. I really enjoyed this project because I was able to take full responsibility and address all of the UX opportunity areas I found. I was able to increase the visual interest, improve the information hierarchy, and add a new navigation system to the Jasper blog all while keeping a minimal, clean interface.

Landing Page Redesign

Hao and I designed a brand new landing page for Jasper. We took on the landing page project because Hao and I were the two patient-track designers on the Jasper UI/UX team — we worked on all products geared towards patients rather than dental offices. To create the landing page, we started with low-fidelity designs, and then made several high-fidelity iterations. We sought feedback from other designers on the UI/UX team and from the two Jasper co-founders.

Finally, we decided on the best design, which you can see below. We chose this landing page design because we wanted the call-to-action for new users to be immediately visible on their screen. We wanted to promote Jasper’s unique user experience — using a quiz to match patients with the perfect dentist — to help distinguish Jasper from competitors.

Demo Request Design

Another individual project I took on at Jasper was designing the demo request screen. This was a quick project that I completed in an afternoon.

Jasper partners with dentist offices to promote the dental practice and provide their patients with financing options for their procedures. Jasper has a healthcare provider dashboard that allows them to schedule appointments, manage patient medical records, and monitor payments. The demo request page on Jasper’s website is for prospective clients to see the provider products in action.
