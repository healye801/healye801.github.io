Jasper Branding and Design Guide

4 weeks of user research, market research, and brand design for Jasper Financial.
 Team: Emma Healy, Hao Xian, Daneeshah Desilva, Jiatong Qing, Jiaheng Wu, Emily Lam, Muriel Chen
 June 2020 - July 2020

Overview

Myself and the UI/UX design interns at Jasper were given the opportunity to develop Jasper’s branding and product design guide as we designed the company's digital products. We conducted user research, competitor research, and then audited the startup’s initial branding efforts. Then, we used these insights to create the new color palette, logo, and typography. Finally, we expanded upon the branding to create Jasper’s product design guidelines.

Landscape Research

First, we compiled all of Jasper’s existing competitor and market research into a slide deck to learn about other companies in healthcare and FinTech. We conducted more landscape research as needed and presented our findings to one of Jasper’s co-founders. Then, we conducted a user survey to better understand how users feel about healthcare financing.

Brand Audit

Next, we evaluated Jasper’s existing brand elements. Because Jasper is such a new company, the original logo, colors, and typography were being used for a dummy website and promotional materials. The original website needed to be completely redesigned and developed, so we had no qualms about updating Jasper’s brand.

Brand Colors, Logo, and Typography

Then, we began to brainstorm new brand elements. I stepped into a project manager role for this project and lead the UI/UX team in establishing a branding calendar, leading meetings, and determining next steps. I presented our branding progress to the Jasper founders each week.

We iterated on logo concepts, narrowed down color palettes, chose the logo font, and picked font pairings. Here is the final brand design that we came up with.

Product Design Guidelines

Finally, we applied our new branding to our low-fidelity product wireframes. The UI/UX team worked together to build out our typography, colors, buttons, and other design elements for mobile and desktop designs.
