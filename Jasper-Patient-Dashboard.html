Jasper Patient Dashboard

10 weeks of research, UX design, and UI design to create a patient dashboard for Jasper Financial.
 Team: Emma Healy, Hao Xian

Overview

Jasper Financial is a brand new startup that helps patients pay for their healthcare. As a UI/UX intern at Jasper, I designed Jasper’s patient-facing products from scratch. Hao Xian and I designed a responsive web app for Jasper users to keep track of their appointments, store their medical and insurance information, access their payment history, and finance their medical bills. After conducting a user survey and landscape research, Hao and I formulated the dashboard’s information architecture, created and iterated on low-fidelity designs, demo-ed our work to the engineering team, and handed off our high-fidelity screens for development. The patient dashboard is being built and will undergo user testing in the Fall of 2020.

Landscape Research

Before we began our design, Hao and I needed to learn the ins and outs of the appointment scheduling and financing process. This research was essential to design a comprehensive, user-friendly product. Financing healthcare procedures is a stressful process, and other financing companies often lack transparency — users feel blindsided or manipulated. Hao and I created a user survey about financing dental procedures, and users said that high prices and hidden costs were very frustrating and even prevented some users from getting dental care.

Based on our user research, I created this persona to guide our design.

Brainstorming

With our user research in mind, Hao and I brainstormed features to include in our dashboard design. We had a lot of ideas — ranging from an AI chatbot for recommending payment plans to a medical form that auto-fills information from a photo of an insurance card — and narrowed them down based on user priorities, time constraints, and feasibility. We met with our PM and Jasper co-founder, Muriel Chen, to make a complete list of features and information to include in the dashboard.

Information Architecture

Next, Hao and I used Mural to create the dashboard’s information architecture. We worked with Jasper’s founder to determine necessary features. We also used the information architecture to map out how the patient dashboard integrates with Jasper’s other products. There are several entry points to the patient dashboard, so the information architecture was very important to work through early in the design process.

Low-fidelity Designs

Then, Hao and I began to create our low-fidelity dashboard design in Figma. We went through many iterations of the design after meeting with the engineering team, receiving critiques from the other UI/UX designers, and accounting for several different use cases for each feature.

Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Some users will only need to make appointments for themselves, but other account holders need to schedule appointments and store information for their children or other dependents.

When we first wireframed the booking process, we created a simpler flow that assumed the user wanted to book an appointment for themselves. In reality, the Jasper account holder will need to make appointments for dependents or other patients. We had to revise our wireframes to account for these users.

In our high-fidelity design, users are able to change the patient for each appointment at multiple checkpoints in the booking process. When the user wants to review their upcoming appointments, they are able to filter by the patient.

The user is also able to save their dependents’ medical forms and other important information in their patient dashboard. Each form is filed under the patient’s name for easy retrieval.

Solution: Additional features that allow users to make appointments for other people, and a patient profile section with space for multiple dependents.
Challenge 2: Payment plans are confusing, and other companies lose trust when users are hit with hidden fees.

Financial information is often hard to understand, and it’s easy to lose important information on a cluttered screen or page. In our survey, users expressed frustration with the lack of transparency in financing companies. Jasper wants to greatly improve the financing user experience, and we designed an interface that is simple and straightforward enough to relieve some of these headaches.

We went through several iterations of the payments tab. You can see how we improved our wireframes to get to the final product.

Solution: A scannable, easily understood payment plan tab that users aren’t afraid of.

High-fidelity Designs

After many iterations of our low-fidelity designs, Hao and I were ready to work on the dashboard’s interface. Our high-fidelity designs needed to be friendly, clean, and scannable. The dashboard holds a lot of information, so it was important that we divided it into easy-to-digest sections that made sense both for desktop and mobile devices. We collaborated with the rest of the UI/UX design team to establish Jasper’s design guidelines and built a component library for future Jasper products.

The final patient dashboard is currently being developed and will undergo user testing in the Fall of 2020.
