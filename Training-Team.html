Establishing an Enterprise Onboarding and Technical Enablement Team

I had an idea, pitched to stakeholders, and won buy-in for a brand new product team to support training.

Role:

UX Designer & Strategic Initiative Lead

Team:

Cross-functional program with 10+ product teams

Tools:

Matomo, Databricks, User Research, Focus Groups

Skills:

Strategic thinking, user research, cross-functional leadership, presentation

Challenge

I was the product designer for a feature-heavy CMS application, working within a program of 10+ product teams all assigned to different enterprise applications. My CMS application had a steep learning curve with complex workflows, creating barriers to user success in a high-stakes government environment with real human downstream impacts.

As I began investigating usability issues specific to my application, I discovered a much larger problem. All 10+ product teams were working independently to improve their respective enterprise applications, with users constantly receiving updates and improvements across the entire suite. However, there was no dedicated technology team for training and enablement across the program, and no centralized resources or information-sharing to keep stakeholders and users informed about development changes.

What started as diagnosing a training problem for my CMS application revealed broader systemic issues affecting the entire suite of enterprise applications.

Key challenges identified:

01.

Feature discoverability crisis - Users repeatedly requested features that had already been shipped

02.

Steep learning curve - Complex workflows created barriers to productivity

03.

Fragmented information - No centralized training resources across 10+ independent teams

04.

High-stakes environment - Government client with downstream human impacts made user competency critical

Opportunity

Opportunity

Through metrics tracking and observational research, I realized that training and adoption was a huge problem not just for my application, but across the entire program. Users were constantly getting updates and improvements, but had no way to stay current with rapidly evolving tools.

This presented an opportunity to solve a systemic organizational problem while creating scalable infrastructure for long-term user success across all product teams.

So we made a research plan.

Research

Research

I created a comprehensive research approach that combined quantitative data analysis with qualitative user insights. I developed the research plan, conducted user sessions, and synthesized findings to build a compelling case for organizational change.

Role:

Lead researcher and moderator

Timeline:

Historical research analysis and targeted user sessions

Methods:

Metrics analysis, observational research, user surveys, focus group

Deliverables:

Leadership presentation with data-driven recommendations

Key Research Findings

Users lacked awareness of existing features. In a room full of users, when I asked who knew how to access foundational features in my application, few people raised their hands.

Feature requests revealed adoption gaps. In surveys and town halls, users repeatedly asked for features that we had already shipped and were available in the interface.

The problem was program-wide. Through metrics tracking and user feedback, I discovered that training challenges extended far beyond my CMS application to the entire program ecosystem.

No dedicated resources existed. There was no dedicated technology team for training and enablement across the program, and no centralized system for knowledge sharing.

Solution

Solution

We developed a comprehensive approach that addressed training challenges at the organizational level:

01.

Identified the root cause - Gathered compelling metrics and user feedback to demonstrate the scope of the problem

02.

Pitched lean, strategic solution - Initially proposed a track of work within my CMS team to develop scalable training features

03.

Advocated for dedicated resources - Presented data showing this was a program-wide issue requiring dedicated team focus

Training

Establishing the New Training Team

Leadership recognized that I had uncovered a much larger organizational problem. Instead of limiting the solution to one team, they decided to establish an entirely new product team dedicated to training and enablement across the entire program.

This was one of the first dedicated training and enablement teams in the client's ecosystem, making it critical to establish clear priorities and create scalable frameworks for user success.

I was selected to lead the newly formed team through a comprehensive 3-month discovery and framing period. This involved:

  • Conducting extensive user research to understand pain points across all 10+ product teams
  • Establishing priorities for the new team based on user needs and business impact
  • Defining the strategic direction for training and enablement solutions
  • Creating frameworks for scalable, reusable training components

Results

Results

The initiative delivered significant organizational change and established sustainable infrastructure for user success:

New team formation: Successfully advocated for and helped establish a dedicated product team focused entirely on training and enablement.

Rapid LMS development: The team shipped a comprehensive Learning Management System in under three months.

Leadership buy-in Achieved complete leadership support for ongoing investment in user training and adoption.

Sustainable infrastructure Established long-term systems for user enablement and knowledge sharing across the organization.

Impact

Impact

Leading this strategic initiative was a major responsibility that demonstrated the power of identifying systemic problems and advocating for comprehensive solutions at the organizational level. I made impactful decisions ranging from research methodology to team priorities to strategic direction.

Key Achievements

01.

Successfully pitched leadership to create an entirely new product team

02.

Established scalable training infrastructure for 10+ cross-functional teams

03.

Created sustainable frameworks for ongoing user enablement across the program

This project demonstrated my ability to think strategically beyond individual product problems, conduct research that drives organizational change, and lead cross-functional teams through complex discovery processes that balance user needs with business constraints and technical realities.
